ABOUT THE BOOK

Bug bounty programs are structured security initiatives that reward independent researchers for responsibly identifying vulnerabilities in real-world systems. Over the past three decades, these programs have fundamentally reshaped how organizations collaborate with external security talent to improve software resilience at scale.

From Netscape's 1995 initiative to the U.S. Department of Defense's "Hack the Pentagon" in 2016, this guide traces the full evolution — and equips you to participate effectively.
